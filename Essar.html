Essar Ports

Chief Financial Officer - Ports Vertical (from August 19 till October 20)

Responsible for entire ports vertical consisting of four operating ports at Hazira (Gujarat), Pradeep (Orissa), Salaya (Gujarat) and Vizag with a total capacity of 110 MMTPA and combined Turnover of approx. 3800 cr. The Group wants to achieve a valuation of USD 4 Billion (currently USD 2.5 Billion) and monetize these assets. We have identified the areas of Interest cost reduction including refinancing/OTS/ECB, renegotiating long term contracts with our captive customers (Arcelor Mittal and Essar Power Gujarat Ltd) with Take or Pay arrangement, Permission to handle Third party cargo and entering long term contracts for same and overall cost reduction through mechanization. We have so far achieved a valuation of USD 3 billion and we should be able to reach USD 4 billion by FY 23. Following is progress so far:

Hazira Port (50 MMTPA):

  • Rating upgrade from CRISIL and India Rating to A neutral from earlier BB+ which helped to reduce Interest (including Penal Interest) from 13.5% to 9.15% p.a on a loan of USD 200 Mn.
  • Applied for approval to handle third party cargo under the Gujarat government port policy which is currently under review. We are in parallel mechanizing the Third party Cargo handling to enhance our capacity from 54 MMTPA to 70 MMTPA in next 3 years.
  • Successfully settled all major disputes with Arcellor Mittal (AMNS) and ensured smooth transition from Essar Steel with continuity of Take or Pay Agreement and contract date.

Salaya (13 MMTPA):

  • ICA signed by Lenders for total outstanding of USD 200 Mn (including interest of USD 30 Mn) in Jan 20 which is now valid till Jan 21. Loan was in default since April, 18. Rate of Interest now capped to 11% and is proposed to be brought down to 8.75%.
  • Renegotiating contract terms with Essar Power Gujarat Limited (our captive customer) to ensure we can successfully restructure facility and their lenders in their financial restructuring approve our contractual terms.
  • Applied under the new port policy of Gujarat Government to get permission to handle any quantity of third party cargo without linkage to Captive cargo volumes.
  • Reduced our power cost by 20%

Paradip Port (12 MMTPA):

  • Loan book of USD 400 Mn out of which consortium loan of 110 Mn will be paid by Oct, 20 from internal accruals and the balance loan which is ECB from Bank of Baroda will be settled as an OTS.
  • Renegotiated contractual terms including Take or Pay agreement with Arcelor Mittal and signed new contract valid till 2025.
  • We will get a rating of A neutral by Dec 20.

Vizag Port (18 MMTPA):

  • Achieve cargo run rate of 16 MMTPA and generate sufficient cash flows to repay debt of USD 110 Mn by 2028.
  • Cost reduction by 15%.

We are also in touch with VTB to completely refinance Hazira, Paradip and Salaya at an Interest cost of under 5% (Libor + 450 basis points).
